Programs & Operating Model

Readiness support that protects primary missions.

TASK L3C strengthens preparedness before disasters — so frontline organizations can do their core work without starting from zero.

Principle

We do not replace missions — we reinforce them

Every organization has a primary mission. TASK L3C is built to strengthen disaster readiness without competing with or distracting from that mission. We support Japanese NGOs and NPOs so their core work remains protected and sustainable across the disaster cycle.

Program pillars

Core functions

Pre‑disaster funding

TASK L3C focuses on securing and directing support before a crisis, enabling partners to:

  • Stabilize staffing and critical operations ahead of emergencies.
  • Prepare equipment, communications, and safe spaces in advance.
  • Run small-scale pilots that can be scaled during real events.

Capacity building

Capacity support helps organizations plan and train for the realities of the disaster cycle:

  • Role definition across before/during/after phases.
  • Scenario planning and tabletop exercises with partner groups.
  • Readiness toolkits grounded in lived experience in Japan.

Mutual support & bridging

TASK L3C acts as a bridge between local Japanese organizations and international supporters:

  • Helping NGOs and NPOs find each other and collaborate.
  • Clarifying expectations and cultural context across borders.
  • Ensuring that support reaches those closest to risk.

Long‑term resilience

Beyond immediate response and recovery, readiness must stay alive through:

  • Ongoing partnerships rather than one-time donations.
  • Community‑informed priorities.
  • Learning continuity across multiple events in Japan.
