Programs & Functions

How TASK L3C strengthens disaster readiness in Japan.

Each program is designed to support Japanese NGOs and NPOs before, during, and after major disasters—so critical work does not start from zero.

Program areas

Core program pillars

Pre-disaster funding

TASK L3C works to secure and direct funding to Japanese NGOs and NPOs before a crisis, enabling them to:

  • Stabilize staffing and critical operations ahead of emergencies.
  • Prepare equipment, communications, and safe spaces in advance.
  • Run small-scale pilots that can be scaled during real events.

Capacity building

Many organizations know what needs to be done but lack the resources to plan and train for it. Capacity-building support includes:

  • Role definition across the disaster cycle (before, during, after).
  • Scenario planning and tabletop exercises with partner groups.
  • Practical guidance drawn from lived experience in Japan.

Mutual support & bridging

TASK L3C acts as a bridge between local Japanese organizations and international supporters:

  • Helping like-minded NGOs and NPOs find each other and collaborate.
  • Clarifying expectations and cultural context across borders.
  • Ensuring that support reaches those closest to people at risk.

Long-term resilience

Beyond immediate response and recovery, communities need long-term support to remain ready for the next event. TASK L3C supports:

  • Ongoing partnerships rather than one-time donations.
  • Community-informed priorities instead of outside assumptions.
  • Continuity of learning across multiple events in Japan.
Next steps
