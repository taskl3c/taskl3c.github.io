Overview

What is TASK L3C?

TASK L3C is a public-benefit organization created to strengthen Japan’s disaster readiness before the next major event by connecting international supporters with frontline Japanese NGOs and NPOs. We help build preparedness, capability, and long-term resilience where it is needed most.

We focus on the “missing layer” between everyday operations and emergency response—supporting planning, training, and funding ahead of time so Japanese organizations are not starting from zero when the worst happens.

Context

Why TASK L3C exists

Japan experiences major disasters with predictable frequency, yet frontline NGOs and NPOs are often forced to respond without pre-disaster funding, mutual support, or international preparation already in place. When a crisis strikes, Japan must respond immediately—often without recognized resources or operational support ready to go.

TASK L3C exists to change this pattern by strengthening readiness before the next event and by connecting committed supporters to the organizations closest to communities at risk.

What we do

Core functions of TASK L3C

Pre-disaster funding

TASK L3C strengthens readiness before an event by providing support to Japanese NGOs and NPOs so no one is starting from zero when disasters strike.

Capacity building

We help frontline organizations train, plan, and define roles across the disaster cycle—before, during, and after major events.

Mutual support & bridging

TASK L3C links local Japanese organizations with international supporters, strengthening coordination, communication, and shared understanding across borders.

Long-term resilience

We remain engaged beyond the immediate crisis—supporting communities as they recover, rebuild, and prepare for the future.

Who we serve

The people TASK L3C is built for

Japanese NGOs & NPOs

  • Join a pre-disaster support network
  • Strengthen capacity before emergencies
  • Participate in training, tools, and role-mapping
  • Build readiness that protects the communities you serve

Donors & partners

  • Support readiness before major events
  • Engage through clear, reliable communication
  • Strengthen essential Japanese NGOs and NPOs
  • Contribute to long-term resilience across Japan
Governance

Transparency is a must

TASK L3C is governed by strict accounting practices supported by digital recording and reporting. Supporters will be able to see when funds move, which organizations receive them, and how parts of Japan’s disaster cycle are being strengthened.

Future functionality includes donor views by disaster phase, organization type, and region in Japan—so supporters can understand at a glance how readiness is growing over time.

Leadership

About the founder

Carl H. Williams is a retired U.S. Air Force Master Sergeant and former IBM Disaster Recovery Specialist with decades of experience supporting Japan’s disaster response ecosystem.

After self-deploying during the 2011 Tohoku disaster, he helped deliver lifesaving programs across Fukushima, Miyagi, and Iwate while working alongside local and international NGOs and NPOs to strengthen operations.

TASK L3C continues that commitment by building readiness before the next major event and by giving supporters a clear way to stand with Japan’s most at-risk communities.
